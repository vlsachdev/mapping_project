Missing speed bumps in urban residential areas

How lack of planning while creating roads in residential areas creates potential for vehicular accidents?

by Vineet Sachdev

Road accidents are the largest cause of accidental death in India. Data from the India’s National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) report on Accidental deaths and suicides in India for 2020 states that road accidents resulted in 36 percent of accidental deaths that happened in 2020.The report also states that most of the road accidents in urban areas are reported at places near residential areas. Such accidents comprised around 32% of all road accidents taking place in urban areas in last 3 years.

Among other reasons for such accidents in urban areas, a major cause is lack of speed breakers on main roads that pass through residential areas. 74% of road accidents in 2020 took place due to over speeding as per a report on road accidents in India from Ministry of road transport and highways.

The map shows a small residential locality in Brookfield area of Bengaluru. It depicts five such road intersections where a speed breaker is missing on main roads or major lanes where a residential area lane joins them. Since most of these residential lanes have buildings from the very beginning, this creates potential for vehicular accidents as a blind spot is created




A population projection report by the National commission on population,Ministry of health and family welfare in 2020 had estimated that the urban population in India would increase from around 31% of total pouplation in 2011 to 35% in 2022.With the expected rise in urban population, proper planning while laying urban roads with speedy bumps might be useful to lower road accidents in residential areas.
